The winds of time are shifting sands,
Nothing is held within our hands.
The future is a veil, unknown,
Yet seeds of bitterness are sown.
Why do we fight? Why do we break
The very hearts we promised to take?
​No soul possesses a divine right
To cast a shadow, to dim the light,
To vent their anger, sharp and cold,
On a spirit they were meant to hold.
A woman is not a shore for every tide,
A place where all life’s storms should hide.
​She is not a target for the fray,
Or a debt that’s meant for him to pay.
Respect is not a gift or a whim,
But the light that should begin with him.
For in a world where we control so little,
And the threads of life are thin and brittle,
​The only thing we truly own
Is the kindness that we have shown.
So let the anger fall away,
And choose a gentler path Today.
